UNIT INFO

These are human-readable names for units, the listing of units that
have two versions for male and female humans (and/or zombies) visible
in them, the weapons that each unit has, and the number that is
assigned to each weapon. The names go from, for example, Infantry_P to
Bazooka, Plane_S to Fighter Jet and Tank_S to AA Tank, but you can call
them whatever you want. For weapon numbers, Weapon 0 is meant to be
usable as a default, while Weapon 1 may be more powerful but only usable
as part of a specific action. AA is short for Anti-Air when it shows up.

In this version, there are now different variants on the same unit kind;
some of these are marked with the prefix "Zombie_" for the walking dead
or their battle-damaged vehicles, where any visible pilot has (in the
default palette) gray-green skin, bright-green insides exposed by gaping
wounds, and red eyes, while the bodies of soldiers and all parts of
vehicles have missing chunks. The suffix "_Alt" marks a female pilot of a
vehicle or a complete replacement of a male foot-soldier with a female
one; the non-alt images have either no human visible or have a male
soldier in them. The differences are usually slight between male and
female soldiers, with a small difference in chest shape, a different pose
for legs that is meant to suggest broader hips (only when the legs are
standing, and not, for instance, crouching to fire a weapon), changes to
head shape (a bit longer hair with a bit more "volume", smaller chin,
smaller mouth, but the same colors for skin, eyes, lips, etc.; these are
soldiers after all, and lipstick is not typically rationed out in times of
war). There are also male and female versions of zombies, where the female
zombies have both the "Zombie_" prefix before the unit name and the "_Alt"
suffix after the name. Female zombies have a slightly different mouth
shape than the male zombies because the smaller chin made the low, wide
"mid-groan" mouth of the male zombies stretch too far outside the bounds
of the face, and they instead have more of a "baring teeth", "screeching",
or "hissing" expression rather than a "groaning" or "roaring" one. Male and
female zombies also have different head wounds, which may be the only way
to distinguish the two types when just the head of a pilot is visible.

There are "Alt" versions of the alien palettes as well as the humans,
but they may look somewhat strange, since the alien palettes render hair
as if it is bald skin, and the female soldiers have slightly longer hair
than the male ones. This makes it look like they have extra skin coming
off of their heads (this may be good for your aliens; it's up to you).
This also means the bald palettes for humans will look strange when
applied to female ("Alt") units, since it will look like they're human
except for a fold of skin coming off the back of their heads.

Units with an exclamation mark (!) instead of a colon (:) between the
first two columns have a Super version available, which is twice the size,
and produces bigger attacks as well. If they have an asterisk (*) instead
of a colon or exclamation mark, that means they can have an Alt version,
but can't have a Super version.

NOTE: Earlier versions called the units now called Truck, Truck_P,
etc. "Supply" instead of Truck, which wasn't very descriptive of
what they actually looked like. So now you have Trucks.

These are the receive strengths to use when a given unit attacks with a weapon.
Mostly, the explosion size or the amount of sparks flying gets bigger as
strength goes up. But for some, like Rockets, it doesn't change size, but rather
timing -- the Infantry_P's Rocket is timed so that with a strength of 3, it hits
at the right time.  Receive animations use the palette of the attacker, or 0 if
there isn't an animation available in your palette.  They work well if the defender
is facing in the direction of the attack (ready to counterattack, in some games).
